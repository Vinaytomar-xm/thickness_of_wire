THEORY

Setup

A laser is directed at a thin wire. By Babinet's Principle, the wire's diffraction pattern equals that of a slit of the same width.

First Minimum Condition

d · sin θ = λ

Working Formula

With D = wire-to-screen distance, b = half-width of central maxima, small angle: sin θ ≈ b/D:

d = D × λ / b
📌 b ∝ D — As screen distance increases, the entire diffraction pattern physically spreads wider. You can directly see this in the simulation: move D slider and watch the fringes expand or contract on the screen.

Why d_calc varies per reading?

Real b measurements have ±1–3% error (scale resolution). Different D readings give slightly different b → slightly different d_calc. Mean of all readings gives the best estimate of true d.

Intensity Distribution

I(θ) = I₀ · (sinβ/β)²   β = πd sinθ / λ

PROCEDURE

1
Select laser source (He-Ne default). Select Wire 1. Switch ON laser.
2
Set D = 700 mm. Observe diffraction pattern on screen — central bright band, dark fringes on sides. Notice pattern is narrow at small D.
3
Click [+ Add Reading]. b is auto-calculated with small noise.
4
Move D to 900, 1100, 1300, 1500 mm. Watch pattern SPREAD wider as D increases. Add each reading.
5
Click [▶ Calculate Mean d] — d_calc fills for each row, mean d shown below with σ.
6
Repeat for all 5 wires. Thinner wire = larger b = wider pattern.
7
Graph tab: plot b vs D — straight line, slope = λ/d → verify d independently.

Precautions

1. Never look directly into the laser beam.

2. Keep room dark for clearer fringe visibility.

3. Measure b from exact centre of central bright fringe to first dark fringe.
